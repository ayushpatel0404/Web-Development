Conjunctions

A conjunction is a part of speech that connects words, phrases, or clauses within a sentence. It is used to join ideas, show relationships between words or groups of words, and create more complex sentences.

Coordinating Conjunctions

Definition:

Coordinating conjunctions are used to join words, phrases, or independent clauses of equal importance within a sentence. There are seven coordinating conjunctions:

  • For - indicates reason or purpose
  • And - indicates addition or similarity
  • Nor - indicates negation or alternative
  • But - indicates contrast or exception
  • Or - indicates choice or alternative
  • Yet - indicates contrast or concession
  • So - indicates result or consequence

Examples:

I like to read books and watch movies.

She didn't want to go to the party, but her friends convinced her.

Subordinating Conjunctions

Definition:

Subordinating conjunctions are used to join an independent clause (main clause) with a dependent clause (subordinate clause) to create a complex sentence. They show the relationship between the main clause and the subordinate clause, and they introduce dependent clauses that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence.

Examples:

I will go to the store if I have enough money.

She couldn't sleep because it was too noisy.

Correlative Conjunctions

Definition:

Correlative conjunctions are used in pairs to connect and balance two or more items within a sentence. They work in pairs and join words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance.

Examples:

Either you come to the party, or you stay at home.

Both John and Mary are going to the concert.

Conjunctive Adverbs

Definition:

Conjunctive adverbs are used to connect two independent clauses within a sentence. They show the relationship between the two clauses, such as contrast, cause and effect, or sequence of events. They are usually preceded by a semicolon and followed by a comma.

Examples:

She studied hard for the exam; however, she didn't do well.

He finished his work; therefore, he went home early.

Conjunctions are important in creating meaningful and complex sentences. They help to connect ideas, show relationships, and convey meaning in written and spoken language.
