Adverbs

  • Adverbs are words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They provide information about how, when, where, or to what extent an action or quality occurs.

  • Adverbs can answer questions such as how (e.g., quickly),when (e.g., yesterday), where (e.g., here), and to what extent (e.g., very).

  • Adverbs can be used to add more detail or description to a sentence. For example:
    • He ran quickly. (The adverb "quickly" modifies the verb "ran" and provides information about how the action was done.)
    • The sun shines brightly. (The adverb "brightly" modifies the verb "shines" and provides information about how the action is done.)
    • She speaks softly. (The adverb "softly" modifies the verb "speaks" and provides information about how the action is done.)
    • They arrived late. (The adverb "late" modifies the verb "arrived" and provides information about when the action occurred.)

  • Adverbs can also modify adjectives and other adverbs. For example:
    • He is extremely tall. (The adverb "extremely" modifies the adjective "tall" and provides information about the extent of the quality.)
    • She sings very well. (The adverb "very" modifies the adverb "well" and provides information about the extent of the quality.)

  • Adverbs can be formed by adding "-ly" to adjectives. For example:
    • Quick (adjective) becomes quickly (adverb)
    • Beautiful (adjective) becomes beautifully (adverb)
    • Happy (adjective) becomes happily (adverb)
